Vedic & Astrometric Computations

1. Drik Siddhanta — Zero Dependencies
All calculations are performed entirely client-side using Jean Meeus algorithms with 22-term lunar perturbations and Lahiri Ayanamsa for sidereal positions. No external APIs are called.
2. Tithi & Nakshatra End Times
Tithi and Nakshatra end times are computed by iteratively scanning forward from the current moment to find when the relevant angular threshold is crossed. The Moon's longitude uses major perturbation terms including evection, variation, and annual equation.
3. Amrit Kaalam
Amrit Kaalam is computed from the Choghadiya system. Each day/night is divided into 8 equal segments, and the segment ruled by the Moon (Amrit) is identified based on the weekday.
4. Dinamana & Kaalams
Daylight (Dinamana) is the span between Sunrise and Sunset. This is divided into 8 segments for Rahu Kaalam, Yama Gantam, and Gulika Kaalam mapping by weekday.
