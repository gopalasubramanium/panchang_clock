Vedic & Astrometric Computations

1. Drik Siddhanta & Zero Dependencies
This dashboard relies on zero external APIs. It calculates precise orbital mechanics entirely client-side using algorithms by Jean Meeus. By computing the True Longitude of celestial bodies based on local coordinates, it adheres strictly to modern observational accuracy (Drik Siddhanta) rather than ancient static constants (Surya Siddhanta).
2. Tithi & Nakshatra Mechanics
A Tithi (Lunar Day) advances exactly when the angular distance between the Sun and Moon increases by 12 degrees. A Nakshatra (Lunar Mansion) changes as the Moon passes through 13°20' segments of the ecliptic. This system uses an embedded algebraic expansion to approximate these true degrees dynamically.
3. Dinamana (Daylight) & Kaalams
Vedic timekeeping defines daylight (Dinamana) strictly as the span between exact local Sunrise (factoring -0.833° for atmospheric refraction) and Sunset. This span is divided into 8 equal mathematical segments to assign Rahu, Yama, and Gulika Kaalams based on the day of the week, ensuring sub-minute local accuracy over generic printed calendars.
